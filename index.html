The Concept

Welcome to the Echo Chamber, a minimalist data-space where survival depends on influence. Signal Runner is a high-tempo, session-based social deduction game of cyber-espionage.

In each 5-minute match, a single truth—the Signal—is released. Simultaneously, a rogue AI warden, Eris, and hidden human players, the Phantoms, flood the network with convincing fakes—the Noise.

As a Runner, you must identify the Signal and amplify it before time runs out. The core question isn't "is this source credible?" but "is the player telling me this trying to save me or kill me?"

Core Mechanics

The Signal Grid

The game is played on a visual grid of "Data Nodes". Players spend action points in a rapid, visual tug-of-war to find the truth, not read logs. Actions include AMPLIFY, JAM, and SHIELD.

Eris, The Strategic Catalyst

The adversarial AI, Eris, is not random. It acts as a predictable yet formidable "raid boss," targeting influential players and forming consensus to create dramatic, king-slayer moments that test the network's integrity.

The "Ghost" Action

Using ZK-proofs, players can perform one anonymous action per game. Amplify or Jam a node with your identity shielded from all players—and from Eris. This high-stakes move creates incredible moments of doubt and surprise.

AI & The Cypherpunk Ethos

Eris is not a black-box LLM. It's a rule-based, verifiable AI whose logic can be audited. This ensures fairness and makes the AI a strategic opponent, not a random troll.

The game embodies cypherpunk principles of privacy and decentralization. The ZK-powered "Ghost" action provides a tool for privacy, while the long-term vision is a fully trustless, player-owned game running on Solana.
